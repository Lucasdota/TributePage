Nikola Tesla

The father of modern light

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Croatia
  • 1870 - Studied engineering and physics without graduating
  • 1882 - Went to work in Paris for the Continental Edison Company, and, while on assignment to Strassburg in 1883, he constructed, after work hours, his first induction motor
  • 1884 - Immigrated to the United States and sold the patent rights to his system of alternating-current dynamos, transformers, and motors to George Westinghouse
  • 1891 - Invented the Tesla coil, an induction coil widely used in radio technology
  • 1898 - Announced his invention of a teleautomatic boat guided by remote control. When skepticism was voiced, Tesla proved his claims for it before a crowd in Madison Square Garden
  • 1900 - Made what he regarded as his most important discovery, terrestrial stationary waves. By this discovery he proved that Earth could be used as a conductor and made to resonate at a certain electrical frequency
  • 1900 - Began construction on Long Island of a wireless world broadcasting tower, with $150,000 capital from the American financier J. Pierpont Morgan. Tesla claimed he secured the loan by assigning 51 percent of his patent rights of telephony and telegraphy to Morgan. He expected to provide worldwide communication and to furnish facilities for sending pictures, messages, weather warnings, and stock reports. The project was abandoned because of a financial panic, labour troubles, and Morgan’s withdrawal of support. It was Tesla’s greatest defeat
  • 1915 - Was severely disappointed when a report that he and Edison were to share the Nobel Prize proved erroneous
  • 1917 - Was the recipient of the Edison Medal in 1917, the highest honour that the American Institute of Electrical Engineers could bestow
  • 1943 - Died at the age of 87 in New York, United States

"The day science begins to study non-physical phenomena, it will make more progress in one decade than in all the previous centuries of its existence."
